Some Names and Bios of Interesting People

Paul Stamets

Mycologist, author and advocate of bioremediation and medicinal fungi.

"Mushrooms are miniature pharmaceutical factories, and of the thousands of mushroom species in nature, our ancestors and modern scientists have identified several dozen that have a unique combination of talents that improve our health".

Steven Pinker

Cognitive psychologist, linguist, and popular science author. He is a Professor in the Department of Psychology at Harvard University, and is known for his advocacy of evolutionary psychology and the computational theory of mind.

"Human nature is complex. Even if we do have inclinations toward violence, we also have inclination to empathy, to cooperation, to self-control."

Robert Sapolsky

Neuroendocrinologist and author. He is currently a professor of biology, and professor of neurology and neurological sciences and, by courtesy, neurosurgery, at Stanford University.

“Depression is not generalized pessimism, but pessimism specific to the effects of one's own skilled action.”

Howard Zinn

Historian, playwright, and social activist. He was a political science professor at Boston University. Zinn wrote more than twenty books, including his best-selling and influential A People's History of the United States.

“Historically, the most terrible things - war, genocide, and slavery - have resulted not from disobedience, but from obedience.”

George Carlin

Stand-up comedian, actor, author, and social critic.Carlin was noted for his black comedy and reflections on politics, the English language, psychology, religion, and various taboo subjects.

"Have you ever noticed that anybody driving slower than you is an idiot, and anyone going faster than you is a maniac?"

John Taylor Gatto

Author and former school teacher who taught in the classroom for nearly 30 years. Authored several books on modern education, criticizing its ideology, history, and consequences.

“The primary goal of real education is not to deliver facts but to guide students to the truths that will allow them to take responsibility for their lives.”

Frank Zappa

Musician, composer, activist and filmmaker. His work is characterized by nonconformity, free-form improvisation, sound experiments, musical virtuosity, and satire of American culture.

"Politics is the entertainment branch of industry."

Aliester Crowley

English occultist, ceremonial magician, poet, painter, novelist, and mountaineer. He founded the religion of Thelema, identifying himself as the prophet entrusted with guiding humanity into the Æon of Horus in the early 20th century.

"Do what thou wilt shall be the whole of the law."

John Dee

English mathematician, astronomer, astrologer, occult philosopher, and advisor to Queen Elizabeth I. He devoted much of his life to the study of alchemy, divination, and Hermetic philosophy.

"Perspective is an Art Mathematical which demonstrates the manner and properties of all radiations direct, broken and reflected."

Terence McKenna

Ethnobotanist, mystic, psychonaut, lecturer, and author. He spoke and wrote about a variety of subjects, including psychedelic drugs, plant-based entheogens, shamanism, and culture.

“If the words ‘life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness’ don’t include the right to experiment with your own consciousness, then the Declaration of Independence isn’t worth the hemp it was written on.”

Ayn Rand

Russian-American novelist, philosopher, playwright, and screenwriter. She is known for her two best-selling novels, The Fountainhead and Atlas Shrugged, and for developing a philosophical system she called Objectivism..

"A creative man is motivated by the desire to achieve, not by the desire to beat others."

Buckminster Fuller

Architect, systems theorist, author, designer, and inventor. Fuller published more than 30 books, coining or popularizing terms such as "Spaceship Earth", ephemeralization, and synergetic. He also popularized the widely known geodesic dome.

"We are not going to be able to operate our Spaceship Earth successfully nor for much longer unless we see it as a whole spaceship and our fate as common. It has to be everybody or nobody."

Bertrand Russell

British philosopher, logician, mathematician, historian, writer, social critic, political activist and Nobel laureate.

"The good life is one inspired by love and guided by knowledge."

John Lilly

Physician, neuroscientist, psychoanalyst, psychonaut, philosopher, writer and inventor.He was a researcher of the nature of consciousness using mainly isolation tanks, dolphin communication, and psychedelic drugs, sometimes in combination.

“For when it starts feeling like a prison in there—and it usually does for most people—you are confronted with the fact that the bars are of your own making.”

Alan Watts

British philosopher, writer, and speaker, best known as an interpreter and populariser of Eastern philosophy for a Western audience..

"The only way to make sense out of change is to plunge into it, move with it, and join the dance."

Christoper Hitchens

Author, columnist, essayist, orator, religious and literary critic, social critic, and journalist. Hitchens was the author, co-author, editor or co-editor of over 30 books, including five collections of essays on culture, politics and literature.

“Human decency is not derived from religion. It precedes it.”

Graham Hancock

British writer and reporter. Hancock specialises in pseudoscientific theories involving ancient civilisations, stone monuments or megaliths, altered states of consciousness, ancient myths, and astronomical or astrological data from the past.

“I don't believe that consciousness is generated by the brain. I believe that the brain is more of a reciever of consciousness.”

.

Ken Kesey

Novelist, essayist, and countercultural figure. He considered himself a link between the Beat Generation of the 1950s and the hippies of the 1960

"People don't want other people to get high, because if you get high, you might see the falsity of the fabric of the society we live in."

Michel Foucault

French philosopher, historian of ideas, social theorist, and literary critic. Foucault's theories primarily address the relationship between power and knowledge, and how they are used as a form of social control through societal institutions.

“I don't feel that it is necessary to know exactly what I am. The main interest in life and work is to become someone else that you were not in the beginning.”

Niccolò Machiavelli

Italian diplomat, politician, historian, philosopher, humanist, and writer of the Renaissance period. He has often been called the father of modern political science. He was for many years a senior official in the Florentine Republic, with responsibilities in diplomatic and military affairs. He also wrote comedies, carnival songs, and poetry.

“Everyone sees what you appear to be, few experience what you really are.”

Henry David Thoreau

American essayist, poet, philosopher, abolitionist, naturalist, tax resister, development critic, surveyor, and historian. A leading transcendentalist, Thoreau is best known for his book Walden and his essay "Civil Disobedience".

“If you have built castles in the air, your work need not be lost; that is where they should be. Now put the foundations under them.”

Noam Chompsky

Linguist, cognitive scientist, historian, social critic, and political activist. Chomsky is also one of the founders of the field of cognitive science. He is the author of over 100 books on topics such as linguistics, war, politics, and mass media.

“The smart way to keep people passive and obedient is to strictly limit the spectrum of acceptable opinion, but allow very lively debate within that spectrum....”

Soren Kierkegaard

Danish philosopher, theologian, poet, social critic and religious author who is widely considered to be the first existentialist philosopher.He wrote critical texts on organized religion, morality, ethics, psychology, and the philosophy of religion, displaying a fondness for metaphor, irony and parables.

“Life can only be understood backwards; but it must be lived forwards.”

Marshall McLuhan

Canadian professor, philosopher, and public intellectual. His work is one of the cornerstones of the study of media theory. He was a fixture in media discourse in the late 1960s.

“The medium is the message.”

Rubert Sheldrake

English author, and researcher in the field of parapsychology, known for his "morphic resonance" concept. Sheldrake's morphic resonance conjecture posits that "memory is inherent in nature".

“I am more interested in dogs than in dogmas. Obviously,”

Manly P. Hall

Canadian-born author, lecturer, astrologer and mystic. He is best known for his 1928 work The Secret Teachings of All Ages. Over his 70 year career, he gave thousands of lectures, including two at Carnegie Hall, and published over 150 volumes.

“Experiences are the chemicals of life with which the philosopher experiments”

Aldous Huxley

English writer, novelist, philosopher, and prominent member of the Huxley family. The author of nearly fifty books,he was best known for his novels including Brave New World, set in a dystopian future.

“There are things known and there are things unknown, and in between are the doors of perception.”

Cecil Rhodes

Anglo-South African businessman, mining magnate and politician in southern Africa who served as Prime Minister of the Cape Colony from 1890 to 1896. An ardent believer in British imperialism, Rhodes and his British South Africa Company founded the southern African territory of Rhodesia, now Zimbabwe and Zambia.

"To be born English is to win first prize in the lottery of life"

Thomas Hobbes

Hobbes is best known for his 1651 book Leviathan, which established the social contract theory that has served as the foundation for most later Western political philosophy. Hobbes also contributed to a diverse array of other fields.

“God put me on this Earth to accomplish a certain number of things. Right now I'm so far behind that I'll never die."

John Marco Allegro

English archaeologist and Dead Sea Scrolls scholar. He was a populariser of the Dead Sea Scrolls through his books and radio broadcasts. Allegro's books, including The Sacred Mushroom and the Cross, brought him both popular fame and notoriety, and also destroyed his career.

"When the secrets of the cult had to be written down, it was done in the form of codes hidden in folk tales.

Robert Anton Wilson

Author, novelist, essayist, editor, playwright, poet, futurist, and self-described agnostic mystic. Recognized as an Episkopos, Pope, and saint of Discordianism, Wilson helped publicize the group through his writings and interviews.

“The Bible tells us to be like God, and then on page after page it describes God as a mass murderer. This may be the single most important key to the political behavior of Western Civilization.”

Joseph Campbell

Mythologist who worked in comparative mythology and comparative religion. His work covers many aspects of the human experience. Campbell's magnum opus is his book The Hero with a Thousand Faces (1949), in which he discusses his theory of the journey of the archetypal hero found in world mythologies.

“Myth is much more important and true than history. History is just journalism and you know how reliable that is.”

Edward Bernays

Austrian-American pioneer in the field of propaganda, referred to in his obituary as "the father of public relations". Bernays was named one of the 100 most influential Americans of the 20th century by Life magazine.

“Men are rarely aware of the real reasons which motivate their actions.”

Jordan Peterson

Clinical psychologist, cultural critic, and professor of psychology at the University of Toronto. His main areas of study are in abnormal, social, and personality psychology.

“I don't think that you have any insight whatsoever into your capacity for good until you have some well-developed insight into your capacity for evil.”

Sam Harris

Author, philosopher, neuroscientist, blogger, and podcast host. He is a critic of religion and proponent of the liberty to criticize religion.

"Either God can do nothing to stop catastrophes like this, or he doesn't care to, or he doesn't exist. God is either impotent, evil, or imaginary. Take your pick, and choose wisely."

Christopher Ryan

Author best known for co-authoring the book Sex at Dawn (2010). He received a B.A. in English and American literature in 1984, and an M.A. and Ph.D. in psychology from Saybrook University.

“But with trust we can strive to accept even what we cannot understand.”
